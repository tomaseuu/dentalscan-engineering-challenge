# DentalScan Audit

## Overall Experience

Overall, I thought the product was really cool and unique. It is not something you see every day, so it stood out right away. The UI felt clean and easy to follow, and the flow made sense: send a link, complete the scan, get AI results, then connect with a dentist. I also liked that the product can work for dental offices, schools, gyms, employers, and charities!

## What Worked Well

The scan flow was the most interesting part. It moved quickly, gave clear angle steps, and felt real because the AI could detect my face, mouth position, and scan angles. Getting results quickly made it feel useful, and I liked that users can connect with a licensed dentist after the scan.

## What Could Be Better

The scan flow could help users recover better if they make a mistake. After each photo, the app could show a quick “Use this” or “Retake” preview instead of moving on automatically. It could also show progress like “2 angles left.” For harder angles, a small reference image or animation could help!

Some website UX things stood out from the website too. A few cards looked clickable but did not do anything. The logo was not clickable on the login page. Some sections felt repetitive, and the footer still said 2025.

## Technical Considerations

The biggest thing I am worried about is the phone camera. Different phones act differently with focus, lighting, permissions, and screen sizes. Any live feedback should be light and simple so it does not make the camera lag or drain battery.
